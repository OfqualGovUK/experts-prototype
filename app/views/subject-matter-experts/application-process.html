Application process

How is the application evaluated?

Your application will be reviewed on your areas of expertise aligning with the evidence you provide.

In the application you will need to detail:

  • your assessment, teaching, lecturing, training or industry expertise, describing your experience, skills, achievements, activities and responsibilities.
  • your subject or sector expertise, describing your experience and skills by qualification type and level (if applicable).

Applicants must undergo satisfactory reference and identity checks and accept our terms and conditions prior to being appointed as a subject matter expert.

What happens after the application is evaluated?

Your application will be accepted or rejected.

If appointed as a subject matter expert, you may be commissioned as an external contractor to carry out specific projects and assignments.

It is important to note, depending on your area of expertise aligning with Ofqual's or The Department for Education's requirements, commissions are not guaranteed during the contractual period.

As a subject matter expert, you are expected to complete assignments to a high standard and will receive feedback following the completion of each assignment in the following areas:

  • quality of work
  • working independently
  • working to time
  • working to specification
  • professionalism
