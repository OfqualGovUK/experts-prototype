What is an Ofqual subject matter expert?

Ofqual is looking for people across different disciplines to apply to become a subject matter expert.

Subject matter experts work externally from Ofqual supporting work on qualifications and assessments across England. These may include GCSEs, A Levels, T-Levels, Apprenticeships, BTECs, functional skills and vocational qualifications.

Subject matter experts work on a contractual basis when needed and are invaluable to the work Ofqual do to ensure the qualifications that we regulate are fit for purpose, ensuring that the right things can be assessed consistently.

Who should apply?

We are looking for people from a range of backgrounds, such as:

  • an assessor or an examiner
  • have extensive skills and experience in a particular industry
  • a teacher, lecturer, trainer, practitioner, or an academic

For example, a Psychology A Level teacher in a school, a space engineering technician with substantial experience in your field, an exam question writer for an Awarding Organisation, a plumbing apprenticeship end-point assessment assessor.

There is a vast amount of qualifications that need experts to review and we value the range of skills and experience that subject matter experts can provide.

What is the role?

As a subject matter expert for Ofqual, you may be contracted to support the review of qualifications relevant to your field, such as:

  • comment on the content of qualifications and the quality of their assessments
  • provide specialist opinion, professional insight or industry expertise
  • become involved in large scale projects where qualifications are being developed, or respond to ad hoc queries
  • contribute to research projects
  • attend and contribute to panel meetings to discuss a qualification review
  • complete a written report within an agreed time frame

Why should I apply?

This is a great opportunity to:

  • use your professional expertise to help shape and improve the education system
  • be involved in crucial pieces of work that will benefit learners
  • gain an insight into how qualifications are regulated
  • be a representative for your subject or industry

Will I be paid?

Yes - the pay for each assignment is set by the manager contracting the work, with each assignment individually appraised.

The rate will be communicated with you prior to any work you are contracted to undertake.

How is the application evaluated?

All applications are evaluated against the eligibility criteria set out in the Person specification (PDF, 147 KB, 5 pages).

Your application will be reviewed on your areas of expertise aligning with the evidence you've provided. Applicants must undergo satisfactory reference and identity checks and accept our terms and conditions prior to being appointed as a subject matter expert.

What happens after the application is evaluated?

Your application will be accepted or rejected.

If appointed as a subject matter expert, you will be contracted as an external contractor to carry out specific projects and assignments.

It is important to note, depending on your area of expertise aligning with Ofqual's or The Department for Education's requirements, commissions are not guaranteed during the contractual period.

As a subject matter expert, you are expected to complete assignments to a high standard and will receive feedback following the completion of each assignment in the following areas:

  • quality of work
  • working independently
  • working to time
  • working to specification
  • professionalism
