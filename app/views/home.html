What is an Ofqual subject matter expert?

{#

Ofqual is looking for people across different disciplines to apply to become a subject matter expert.

#}

Subject matter experts work externally from Ofqual supporting work on qualifications and assessments across England. These may include GCSEs, A Levels, T-Levels, Apprenticeships, BTECs, functional skills and vocational qualifications.

Subject matter experts work on a contractual basis when needed and are invaluable to the work Ofqual do to ensure the qualifications that we regulate are fit for purpose, ensuring that the right things can be assessed consistently.

Ofqual is looking for people across different disciplines to apply to become a subject matter expert.

Who should apply?

We are looking for people from a range of backgrounds with subject specific expertise. A subject matter expert may have multiple types of expertise and multiple areas of expertise.

Subject matter experts may have experience working with the qualifications we regulate. This may include subjects, apprenticeships or more general industry experience.

Your area of expertise should be at least one subject or sector, and you may have experience in specific qualification types or levels.

For example your area of expertise may be Biology GCSE, Youth Support Worker End-point Assessment, Building Services Engineering for Construction T-Level, Level 3 Award in Pet Services or the engineering sector at levels 3 and 4.

The qualifications that we regulate are published on Ofqual's qualification register.

The types of expertise we are looking for are:

  • Assessment expertise
    You may have experience in designing assessments, verifying marking or researching assessment policy and practice
  • Industry or occupational expertise
    You may have gained extensive experience in your industry, or you may have subject specific knowledge and skills
  • Teaching, lecturing or training expertise
    You may have experience in developing classroom materials, training other staff or strong knowledge of specifications and qualification standards
{#

There is a vast amount of qualifications that need experts to review and we value the range of skills and experience that subject matter experts can provide.

#}

What is the role?

As a subject matter expert for Ofqual, you may be contracted to support the review of qualifications relevant to your field. You will use communication, analytical and judgement skills in the following types of activities:

  • review and report on the content of qualifications and the quality of their assessments
  • provide specialist opinion and expertise to evaluate the validity of qualifications and assessments
  • become involved in large scale projects where qualifications are being developed, or respond to ad hoc queries
  • contribute to research projects
  • attend and contribute to panel meetings to discuss a qualification review
  • complete a written report within an agreed time frame

Why should I apply?

Subject matter experts from industry, assessment and education work with Ofqual to ensure that qualifications meet the required standards and are an accurate measure of what a student or apprentice knows and can do.

As a subject matter expert you can:

  • help shape and improve the education system
  • support and develop current and future qualifications in your sector
  • improve quality, validity and relevance of qualifications that will benefit students and apprentices in your industry
  • gain insight into how qualifications are regulated
  • continue to improve communication, analytical and judgement skills

Will I be paid?

Yes - the pay for each assignment is set by the manager contracting the work, with each assignment individually appraised.

The rate will be communicated with you prior to any work you are contracted to undertake.

How is the application evaluated?

Your application will be reviewed on your areas of expertise aligning with the evidence you've provided.

Applicants must undergo satisfactory reference and identity checks and accept our terms and conditions prior to being appointed as a subject matter expert.

What happens after the application is evaluated?

Your application will be accepted or rejected.

If appointed as a subject matter expert, you may be commissioned as an external contractor to carry out specific projects and assignments.

It is important to note, depending on your area of expertise aligning with Ofqual's or The Department for Education's requirements, commissions are not guaranteed during the contractual period.

As a subject matter expert, you are expected to complete assignments to a high standard and will receive feedback following the completion of each assignment in the following areas:

  • quality of work
  • working independently
  • working to time
  • working to specification
  • professionalism
