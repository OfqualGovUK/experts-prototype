Who can be a specialist

Who should apply?

We are looking for people from a range of backgrounds. Subject matter specialists may have experience working with the qualifications we regulate. This may include experience with academic subjects, vocational subjects, apprenticeships, or you may have industry experience.

You will need to select at least one subject area, and you may have experience in specific qualification types or levels.

For example, your subject area could be teaching Biology GCSE, assessing the Youth Support Worker End-point Assessment, lecturing the Building Services Engineering for Construction T Level, marking the Level 3 Award in Pet Services, or industry experience in Civil Engineering.

The qualifications that we regulate are published on Ofqual's qualification register.

The types of expertise we look for are:

  • Assessment expertise
    You may have experience in designing or developing assessments, verifying marking or researching assessment policy and practice, or quality assurance of assessments
  • Industry, occupational or professional expertise
    You may have gained extensive experience in your industry, or you may have subject specific knowledge and skills
  • Teaching, lecturing or training expertise
    You may have experience in developing classroom materials, training other staff or a strong knowledge of specifications

Why should I apply?

Subject matter specialists from industry, assessment and teaching backgrounds provide advice to support Ofqual in ensuring that the qualifications we regulate are an accurate measure of the skills, knowledge and understanding that a student or apprentice has demonstrated.

As a subject matter specialist you can:

  • support and develop current and future qualifications in your sector
  • improve quality, validity and relevance of qualifications that will benefit students and apprentices in your industry
  • gain insight into how qualifications are regulated
  • continue to improve your communication and analytical judgement skills
